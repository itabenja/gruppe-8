Conclusion

Imagine this: Iceland and Algeria, two countries on opposite sides of the energy spectrum, each with their own story to tell. Iceland, a volcanic powerhouse, taps into its natural geothermal heat and flowing rivers, running almost entirely on renewable energy. Meanwhile, Algeria, surrounded by the Sahara’s golden sands, faces a different challenge. Without those abundant rivers or volcanoes, Algeria turns to its rich fossil fuels—oil and gas—to keep the lights on.
It’s a reminder that geography isn’t just about landscapes; it’s about the resources that shape our world. And let’s not forget policies! How governments choose to embrace energy solutions can make a huge difference, whether it's a shift towards clean energy or sticking with fossil fuels. So, next time you think about renewable energy, remember: where you live can really change the game!
