Why Algeria and Iceland Differ in Renewable Energy Use The stark difference in renewable energy use between Algeria and Iceland highlights the impact of geography, resources, and policies on energy strategies. Iceland benefits from abundant geothermal and hydropower resources due to its volcanic activity and high water availability, enabling it to rely almost entirely on renewable energy. In contrast, Algeria, located in the arid Sahara region, lacks access to significant hydropower or geothermal resources and has historically depended on its vast fossil fuel reserves, like oil and natural gas, for energy. Such disparities reflect broader global trends. Geography determines the availability of renewable resources like wind, solar, and water. Economic priorities, energy infrastructure, and government policies also shape how countries harness and prioritize renewable energy. Together, these factors create significant variations in renewable energy adoption worldwide.
